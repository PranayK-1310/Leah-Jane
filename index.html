My Story

A Girl from the Rock

My name is Leah Jane McKenney and I grew up in St. John's, Newfoundland — a place where music isn't performed, it's lived. From kitchen parties to community gatherings, music was the language of my childhood. It shaped who I am, and I carry those roots with me everywhere I go.

My dad put a guitar in my hands when I was little and we learned together — Bob Marley's Redemption Song and Tom Petty's Free Falling were the first songs we ever played. Those first chords lit a fire in me that never went out. I also fell in love with the piano, and spent years training my ear for melody and harmony.

Driven by a dream bigger than the island I called home, I moved to Oakville, Ontario to study Musical Theatre at Sheridan College — sharpening my craft and expanding my world. Today I'm based in Toronto, writing indie-pop songs layered with folk and rock, making music that I hope transports you somewhere, makes you feel something, makes you remember.

My Journey

How It All Unfolded

Early Life

Kitchen Parties & First Chords

I grew up in St. John's, Newfoundland, surrounded by music at every turn. Back home, people come together and have kitchen parties — music is how we honour each other and celebrate life. It's embedded in me. My dad bought me my first guitar and taught me how to play, and that was it for me. I was hooked.

College Years

Sheridan College — Musical Theatre

I moved away from home to study Musical Theatre at Sheridan College in Oakville, Ontario — one of Canada's best performance programs. Those years were transformative. I trained in acting, dance, and vocal performance, and I started to understand what kind of artist I wanted to be. It was the foundation for everything that came next.

2018

My Screen Debut — Askin' for It

I got my first film credit with Askin' for It — a bold step into screen acting that I'll never forget. It showed me that I could carry emotion and story beyond the stage, and it opened my eyes to a whole new world of creative possibility.

2023

First Writing Camp — Finding My Sound

My very first songwriting camp was a super big moment for me — it was the first time I felt like I truly found a sound I resonated with. That's where I wrote Paper Crown. The whole thing felt magical. I was surrounded by incredible collaborators from the Toronto indie scene and the puzzle pieces just snapped into place.

Jan 2024

My Debut Single — "Paper Crown"

I released Paper Crown through Spooky Action Records — my very first single ever — and I genuinely didn't know what to expect. When people started telling me they listened to it multiple times a day, I just cried. I felt so grateful, like the universe sent me literal angels making my dreams come true. It was everything.

Mar 2024

My Second Single — "Scarlet Letter"

I wrote Scarlet Letter at my kitchen table about a time when I felt used and betrayed — like I'd been vulnerable with someone and it was used against me. Once I wrote the chorus, I knew it had to be a single. Mike Tompa arranged and played the most perfect string section for it, and it still makes me cry to this day.

Summer 2024

My Debut EP — "Nottingham Drive"

This is the record I was born to make. Nottingham Drive is named after a real place woven into my personal story, and it holds all of my most honest songs — the Newfoundland girl, the theatre student, the young woman learning to turn pain into art. Cinematic, emotional, nostalgic. The music I've always dreamed of making.

2024–2025

Back on Screen & New Music — Format, All in Baby, Clearly

I've been busy! I appeared in Format (2024) and All in, Baby (2025), and kept releasing music — Same Cloth, The Fury, and my 2025 single Clearly. I love making art in every form I can. Honestly? This journey has only just begun, and I can't wait to share what's coming next.

What Drives Me

The Soul Behind My Songs

Family & My Roots

My parents are my biggest supporters — they never made me feel like I couldn't do this. My dad bought me my first guitar and believed in me from the start. My granny always told me to look for the magic in life, and I carry that with me every single day. Newfoundland is home in my heart, and I go back to write music there whenever I can.

My Musical Heroes

Taylor Swift's emotional storytelling, Avril Lavigne's indie-punk spirit, Gregory Alan Isakov's folk poetry, Bob Marley's soul — my influences span generations and genres. I gravitate toward music that feels like a movie, that captures a moment in time. I want my songs to do exactly that for the people who listen to them.

Healing Through Songwriting

For me, writing songs isn't performance — it's therapy. Every song I write comes from a real moment: real pain, real love, real growth. Writing Paper Crown was cathartic. Scarlet Letter helped me process a really hard time. I write to understand myself, and I hope that when you listen, you find a little piece of yourself in it too.

Gratitude & My Community

I am so grateful for the collaborators and mentors around me in Toronto. I would not change my team for the world — there's so much love there. I'm currently reading Rick Rubin's The Creative Act and I carry Eleanor Roosevelt's words with me as a personal mantra: "No one can make you feel inferior without your consent."
